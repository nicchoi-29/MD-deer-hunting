Though hunters have been villainized in both My Cousin Vinny and Bambi (and understandably so, for there’s a reason why the expression doe-eyed exists), they play an important role in managing Maryland’s deer population. Concerns over deer range from backyard garden nuisances to larger agricultural disruptions to increased risk of vehicle crashes to the spread of zoonotic tickborne diseases, including but not limited to Lyme.

Relatively high numbers of reported cases each year have placed Maryland in one of the top 10 states for Lyme disease, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

Incidence rates of Lyme disease in Maryland have remained above the national average from 2015 to 2022, based on the most recent data provided through the Johns Hopkins Lyme and Tickborne Diseases Dashboard, with over 2400 new cases reported in 2023, according to the CDC.

And these are just the reported cases. The prognostic bullseye rash associated with Lyme doesn’t appear on everyone. “As many as 40% of Lyme disease cases are not diagnosed until a later stage,” at which point symptoms can be difficult to trace to the disease, according to the Lyme Wellness Initiative.
